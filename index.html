1
Install Homebrew
💡 What:Installing a tool that lets you install other tools from the command line
⚡ Why:macOS doesn't ship with developer tools by default. Homebrew is the universal installer for everything else in this guide.
🎯 How it helps you:You'll never manually download and install developer software again — one command handles everything.

Homebrew is Mac's package manager — think of it as the App Store for developer tools. You'll only ever install it once, and it makes everything else in this guide possible. 

# Paste this into Terminal and press Enter
/bin/bash -c "$(curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Homebrew/install/HEAD/install.sh)"
💡 What to expect This will open Terminal and ask for your Mac password. That's normal — it needs permission to install software. Type your password and press Enter (nothing will appear as you type — that's a security feature).
2
Install the Core Tools

Run each command below in Terminal, one at a time. Wait for each to finish before running the next — you'll know it's done when you see a $ prompt again.

⚡
Node.js The engine that runs OpenClaw — like the electricity that powers the whole system
💡 What:Installing the JavaScript runtime that OpenClaw is built on
⚡ Why:OpenClaw is a Node.js application. Without it, the software simply won't run.
🎯 How it helps you:Once installed, it runs silently in the background — you'll never interact with it directly. 
brew install node
🦊
OpenClaw Your AI assistant software — this installs the main program that runs your AI
💡 What:Installing your AI assistant software
⚡ Why:This is the core program — it connects all your tools, runs your automations, and manages your AI's brain
🎯 How it helps you:After setup, your AI will handle your inbox, prep your meetings, and run overnight tasks automatically 
npm install -g openclaw
🔒
Cloudflared Your secure internet tunnel — lets your AI receive messages without exposing your Mac. Think of it as a secure post box.
💡 What:Creating a secure tunnel between your Mac and the internet
⚡ Why:Your AI needs to receive messages and calls from Discord, WhatsApp, and phone calls. This tunnel lets that happen without exposing your Mac.
🎯 How it helps you:This is what makes your AI reachable 24/7, even on your home or office network 
brew install cloudflared
📧
GOG — Gmail Connector This is how your AI reads and sends emails on your behalf — handles the secure connection to Google
💡 What:Installing the Gmail/Google connector
⚡ Why:Your AI needs authorized, secure access to your Gmail and Google Calendar
🎯 How it helps you:Once connected, your AI can read your calendar before meetings, triage your inbox, and draft replies — all without you lifting a finger 
npm install -g gogcli
☁️
GitHub CLI Your AI's backup system — lets your AI save its memory and work to the cloud overnight
💡 What:Installing your AI's cloud backup tool
⚡ Why:Your AI's memory files, built apps, and notes are backed up to GitHub every night
🎯 How it helps you:If your Mac ever dies or you get a new computer, all your AI's memory and work is restored in minutes 
brew install gh
3
Set Up OpenClaw
💡 What:Running the configuration wizard
⚡ Why:OpenClaw needs to know your API keys, which channels to listen on, and how to connect to your services
🎯 How it helps you:This is the one-time setup that wires everything together. After this, your AI is configured to your account specifically.

Run the setup wizard — it will ask you a few questions and create your AI's config file. It takes about 5 minutes. 

# This runs the setup wizard
openclaw setup
📋 What the wizard asks for The wizard will ask for your API keys — see below for where to get them. Have both ready before you run this command. Setup takes about 5 minutes.
🔑  Where to Get Your API Keys
Anthropic — The AI Brain
console.anthropic.com  → API Keys → Create Key
This is the key that gives your AI its intelligence. JC will set a spending limit on your key so there are no surprises.
Brave Search — Web Access
api.search.brave.com  → Subscribe → Free Tier
This lets your AI search the web. The free tier is plenty for daily use — no payment info needed.
4
Connect Your Gmail
💡 What:Granting your AI secure access to your Gmail account
⚡ Why:Without this, your AI is blind to your inbox and calendar
🎯 How it helps you:Your AI will read your morning schedule, flag urgent emails, and draft replies — you review and approve before anything sends

This connects your Candosa Google account to your AI, so it can read your calendar and help manage your inbox.

Replace the email address with yours before running
# Replace 'yourname@candosa.com' with your actual email
GOG_KEYRING_PASSWORD=YourChosenPassword gog auth add yourname@candosa.com \
  --client jclaw-personal --services gmail,calendar,contacts
🌐 What happens next This opens a browser window asking you to log into your Candosa Google account. Click Allow — your AI will then be able to read your calendar and manage your inbox on your behalf.
🔐 About YourChosenPassword Replace YourChosenPassword with any password you choose. This is a local encryption password for storing your Gmail credentials — it never leaves your machine. Write it down somewhere safe.
5
Set Up Your Discord Workspace
💡 What:Setting up the interface where you communicate with your AI
⚡ Why:Your AI needs a home base — Discord is where it lives, reports, and waits for your instructions
🎯 How it helps you:Instead of switching between tools, everything your AI does surfaces in one place. Your inbox, calendar, deals, intel — all in one server.
🎮 What is Discord? Discord is where you talk to your AI and where it sends you updates. Think of it as your AI's home base — every report, alert, and task output lands here.
1
Create your Discord account (if you don't have one)
  • Go to discord.com → Create an Account
  • Use your Candosa email address
2
Create your own private AI server
  • Open Discord → click the "+" button in the left sidebar → "Create My Own" → "For me and my friends"
  • Name it: "[Your Name]'s AI Workspace" (e.g. "Lorraine's AI Workspace")
  • This is YOUR private server — just you and your AI. Nobody else.
3
Get your server ID
  • Right-click your server name in the sidebar → "Copy Server ID"
    (If you don't see this option: go to Discord Settings → Advanced → turn on Developer Mode)
  • Save this ID — you'll need it when running the setup script
4
Your key channels (auto-created by setup script)
  • #general — Talk to your AI here. Ask anything, give instructions, kick off projects.
  • #morning-brief — Your daily briefing arrives here at 8 AM
  • #inbox — Email drafts and escalations land here
  • #ops-log — What your AI did overnight
5
How to talk to your AI

Just type naturally — no commands, no special syntax:

"What's on my calendar today?" "Draft a reply to the email from [person]" "Prep me for my 3pm call"
📌 Tip: Pin the server to your Discord sidebar. This is your AI's dashboard — it's where everything lives.
✅ Discord is ready! One more step before you launch: give your AI its memory, personality, and skills.
6
Set Up Your AI's Brain
💡 What:Setting up your AI's memory, personality, skills, and nightly maintenance
⚡ Why:An AI without memory forgets everything. Without skills, it can't do complex tasks. Without the dream routine, it never learns.
🎯 How it helps you:After this step, your AI will remember your preferences, automatically triage your inbox, prep your meetings, and get smarter every day it runs.
🧠 What is this step? Your AI is installed, but right now it has no memory, no personality, and no context about who you are or what you do. This step gives it a brain.
A
Clone the Workspace Templates
# This downloads your AI's workspace — the memory system,
# skills library, and all the configuration files that make
# your AI actually useful
git clone https://github.com/jcg8802/candosa-ai-workspace ~/.openclaw/workspace
What / Why / How What: Downloading a pre-built workspace with your AI's memory architecture, skills, and templates.

Why: Without this, your AI has no persistent memory, no skills, and no understanding of Candosa's context.

How: After this step, your AI will remember things across sessions, learn your preferences, and have access to pre-built tools like inbox triage, morning briefs, and meeting prep.
B
Personalize Your AI
# Open these files and replace [PLACEHOLDERS] with your info
# Use any text editor — TextEdit works fine
open ~/.openclaw/workspace/SOUL.md   # Your AI's personality
open ~/.openclaw/workspace/USER.md   # About you — name, role, timezone
open ~/.openclaw/workspace/TOOLS.md  # Your setup specifics
SOUL.md is your AI's personality file — give it a name and tell it what your role is. USER.md tells your AI who you are. These files are the difference between a generic assistant and one that actually knows you.
C
Set Up Memory
# Create your AI's daily memory directory
mkdir -p ~/.openclaw/workspace/memory

# Create your long-term memory file
touch ~/.openclaw/workspace/MEMORY.md
Your AI writes notes to itself every day in the memory/ folder. The MEMORY.md file is its long-term memory — it reads this at the start of every session so it knows what's happened before. This is how your AI gets smarter over time instead of forgetting everything.
D
Set Up World State
# Create the shared intelligence file
mkdir -p ~/.openclaw/workspace/data
touch ~/.openclaw/workspace/data/world-state.md
World state is a shared file that all your AI's agents update with their latest findings. Your market agent writes its signals here. Your inbox agent writes what it found. The morning brief agent reads all of it to give you a connected picture instead of disconnected reports.
E
Install Your Skills
# Your AI's skill library — pre-built tools for common tasks
# Already included in the workspace clone above
ls ~/.openclaw/workspace/skills/
Skills are pre-built workflows your AI follows for specific tasks. The workspace includes: morning-brief (daily briefing), ea-inbox (email triage), dream (nightly memory maintenance), overnight-ops (system health checks), and more. Think of them as apps for your AI.
F
Wire the Dream Routine
# Schedule your AI's nightly maintenance
# This runs at 4:30 AM every night — updates memory, writes session handoff,
# preps you for tomorrow
openclaw cron add --name "Dream Routine" \
  --schedule "30 4 * * *" \
  --tz "America/New_York" \
  --skill dream
The Dream Routine is your AI's nightly maintenance window. While you sleep, it consolidates what happened today into long-term memory, prepares a handoff for tomorrow's session, and checks your calendar for what needs prep. You wake up to an AI that already knows where things stand.
🧠 You're almost there. Your AI now has memory, personality, and skills. One last step: start it up.
7
Start Your AI
💡 What:Launching your AI in the background
⚡ Why:The gateway is the always-on process that keeps your AI listening and responding
🎯 How it helps you:From this point on, your AI is running 24/7 — even when you're asleep, it's processing, learning, and doing work

This starts your AI in the background. It will run quietly even when you close Terminal. Your AI is now live and listening. 

openclaw gateway start

Then verify it's running:

openclaw gateway status
✅ Setup complete! If you see "running" in the output — your AI is fully live. It has memory, personality, skills, and a Discord home. Welcome to the team 🦊
